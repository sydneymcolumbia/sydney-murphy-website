SNDK Earnings Preview: The NAND Supercycle Nobody Is Talking About

Sandisk reports earnings today. The street is focused on the wrong metrics. Here is what actually matters — and why the setup into this print is more interesting than consensus suggests.

The Spinoff Discount Is Still Real

When Western Digital completed the spinoff of Sandisk in 2025, the market did what it always does with newly independent companies: it underpriced it. Institutional funds that held WD had no mandate to own a pure-play NAND storage company. They sold. Index funds hadn't yet rebalanced. The result was a textbook spinoff discount baked into a business with genuinely differentiated assets.

That discount hasn't fully closed. SNDK trades at a meaningful discount to its NAND peers on an EV/Revenue basis, despite having one of the cleanest balance sheet setups in the group post-separation. That gap is the foundation of the thesis — and today's earnings report is the next catalyst to close it.

What the Street Is Getting Wrong

Consensus is laser-focused on near-term NAND spot pricing, which has been volatile. That's the wrong lens. Spot pricing is a lagging signal. What matters now is enterprise SSD contract pricing, which has been firming quietly for two quarters — and SNDK has disproportionate exposure to enterprise relative to its consumer flash peers.

The AI infrastructure buildout is the underlying driver here. Every hyperscaler adding GPU clusters needs a corresponding expansion in high-speed storage. SNDK's enterprise SSD lineup sits directly in that demand path. The market hasn't priced the duration of that tailwind — it's treating SNDK like a cyclical commodity play when the mix shift toward enterprise makes it increasingly a secular growth story.

What I'm Watching in Today's Print

Three numbers will tell the whole story tonight:

1. Enterprise SSD revenue mix. If enterprise as a percentage of total flash revenue continues its upward trend, the multiple re-rate thesis accelerates. Any commentary pointing toward enterprise demand strengthening into the back half of 2026 should be treated as highly significant.

2. Gross margin trajectory. NAND margins were compressed through the oversupply cycle of 2024. The recovery has been underway, but the pace matters. A gross margin print above 30% — and forward guidance holding or improving — would be a meaningful signal that the trough is definitively in the rearview.

3. Management tone on supply discipline. Samsung and SK Hynix have both signaled restraint on NAND capacity additions. If SNDK's management echoes that the competitive environment is rational, the pricing outlook strengthens materially for the next 12 months.

Key Levels to Watch

Bull case reaction (beat + raise)+12% to +18%
Base case (in-line, constructive guide)+3% to +6%
Bear case (miss or cautious guide)-8% to -14%
Enterprise mix threshold (bullish signal)> 45% of flash revenue
Gross margin threshold (inflection signal)> 30%

The Bottom Line

SNDK is one of the more asymmetric setups I've seen heading into an earnings print this year. The spinoff discount provides a structural floor. The enterprise SSD exposure gives it a growth narrative that the market hasn't fully priced. And with NAND supply discipline holding across the industry, the pricing environment heading into the second half of 2026 looks more constructive than the bears are modeling.

I'm watching tonight's print closely. A beat-and-raise with strong enterprise mix commentary would be the confirmation the stock needs to break out of its post-spinoff holding pattern. The signal is there — the market just hasn't caught up yet.
